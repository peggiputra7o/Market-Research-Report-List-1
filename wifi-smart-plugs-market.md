<p><p>The WiFi smart plugs market is divided into three main types based on plug configurations: two-hole plugs, three-hole plugs, and porous plugs. Two-hole plugs are typically used in regions with standard two-prong outlets, ideal for devices with lower power requirements. Three-hole plugs incorporate an additional grounding hole, enhancing safety for heavier appliances. Porous plugs feature multiple openings, allowing for greater versatility in wiring and compatibility with various socket types. This segmentation caters to diverse consumer needs and regional standards.</p></p>
