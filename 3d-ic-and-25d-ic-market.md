<p><p>3D IC (three-dimensional integrated circuits) and 2.5D IC (two-and-a-half-dimensional integrated circuits) are advanced packaging technologies that allow for improved performance and efficiency in semiconductor devices. 3D IC involves stacking multiple silicon dies vertically, interconnected via through-silicon vias (TSVs), enabling shorter interconnect paths and enhanced performance. In contrast, 2.5D IC employs a flat design where multiple dies are mounted side by side on an interposer, facilitating high-speed interconnections without the complexity of vertical stacking.</p><p>The market for 3D IC and 2.5D IC is poised for significant growth, driven by increasing demand for high-performance computing, mobile devices, and data-intensive applications such as AI and cloud computing. The need for enhanced bandwidth and reduced power consumption is pushing manufacturers to adopt these technologies. The market is expected to grow at a CAGR of 4% during the forecast period, reflecting a strong interest in miniaturization and efficiency. Key trends include innovations in packaging materials, the integration of heterogeneous components, and growing investment in research and development. These advancements are helping overcome challenges like thermal management and manufacturing costs, propelling the market forward.</p></p>
