<p><p>The prostatic stent market is divided into two main types: permanent and temporary stents. Permanent stents are designed for long-term placement, providing ongoing relief from urinary obstruction caused by conditions like benign prostatic hyperplasia. They offer a durable solution but may require surgical intervention for removal in some cases. Temporary stents, on the other hand, are intended for short-term use, often employed during acute urinary retention or post-surgery, allowing for flexible treatment options and easier removal when no longer needed.</p></p>
