<p><p>Geiger-Mueller tubes are essential components in radiation detection, categorized mainly into two types: end window and windowless. The end window type features a thin window that allows low-energy beta particles to enter, making it effective for detecting low radiation levels. Conversely, windowless types are designed for measuring higher energy radiations, including gamma rays and alpha particles, as they lack a window barrier. Each type serves distinct applications in monitoring radiation levels across various industries and research fields.</p></p>
