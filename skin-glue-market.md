<p><p>The skin glue market is divided into two main types: synthetic and natural. Synthetic skin glues are chemically formulated adhesives that provide strong bonding, often used in surgical applications for their durability and quick-setting properties. Natural skin glues are derived from biological sources, such as collagen or plant-based materials, offering biocompatibility and reduced risk of allergic reactions. Both types serve critical functions in medical procedures, wound closure, and tissue repair, catering to varying preferences for safety and efficacy.</p></p>
