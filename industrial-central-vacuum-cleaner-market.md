<p><p>The industrial central vacuum cleaner market comprises two primary types: centralized and distributed systems. Centralized systems feature a single, powerful unit located away from the workspace, delivering suction through a network of pipes to multiple inlets. This setup is efficient for large facilities, minimizing noise and maximizing performance. In contrast, distributed systems utilize multiple, smaller vacuum units placed throughout the facility. This approach offers flexibility and easier access for localized cleaning tasks, catering to diverse operational needs across industries.</p></p>
