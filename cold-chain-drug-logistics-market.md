<p><p>Cold chain drug logistics encompasses three primary transportation methods: airways, roadways, and seaways. Airways offer rapid delivery for time-sensitive pharmaceuticals, maintaining precise temperature control during transit. Roadways provide flexible and accessible transportation for last-mile delivery, ensuring that medications reach healthcare facilities efficiently. Seaways are utilized for bulk shipments over longer distances, often incorporating refrigerated containers to sustain required temperatures. Each method plays a crucial role in the safe and effective distribution of temperature-sensitive drugs across global markets.</p></p>
